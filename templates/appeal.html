Appeal against a Small Claims Order

Orders by the Small Claims Tribunals (SCT) are final and binding. The claimant or respondent may appeal against a decision by the Small Claims Tribunals under specific circumstances. Appeals can be filed at 2 stages:

• After a consultation with a registrar
•After a hearing with a tribunal magistrate

If you are not enquiring on behalf of a company or business entity, (including private limited), please note that the On-Site Legal Advice Scheme entitles you to a one-off free legal consultation on the subject-matter. Henceforth, you are strongly encouraged to complete the appeal application via the Community Justice and Tribunals system if your matter falls under their purview. For more information, you may refer to the Judiciary website.
