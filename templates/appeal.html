Appeal against a Small Claims Order

Orders by the Small Claims Tribunals (SCT) are final and binding. The claimant or respondent may appeal against a decision by the Small Claims Tribunals under specific circumstances. Appeals can be filed at 2 stages:

• After a consultation with a registrar
• After a hearing with a tribunal magistrate

For more information, you may refer to the Judiciary website.

If you are not enquiring on behalf of a company or business entity, (including private limited), please note that the On-Site Legal Advice Scheme entitles you to a one-off free legal consultation on the subject-matter. You may obtain a queue number from the ticket-issuing machine at Level 1, Information Counter of the State Courts, which are issued on a first-come, first-served basis.
