Dispute a Small Claims Order

Disputing the claim means you do not agree with it. You must attend the consultation, otherwise, the court may make a default order (i) in your absence.

• Dispute part of the claim
• Dispute the full claim

If you are not enquiring on behalf of a company or business entity, (including private limited), please note that the On-Site Legal Advice Scheme entitles you to a one-off free legal consultation on the subject-matter. Henceforth, you are strongly encouraged to formally notify your intent to dispute the claim via the Community Justice and Tribunals system if your matter falls under their purview. For more information, you may refer to the Judiciary website.
